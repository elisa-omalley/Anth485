Saint Mary the Virgin Cemetery

Saint Mary the Virgin Cemetery

~ how I created a website for this project.

Posted by Elisa O'Malley on October 25, 2017

I originally began this project in February of 2017, for my Archaeology of Death course with Erin Mcguire. I found myself taking the project seriously, and really getting into the research behind the cemetery. In the fall of 2017, I was asked to do a digital anthropology project that had to do with "the bones of our past", and I thought it would be a great opportunity to pick up the Saint Mary's project again.

For the website, I added a map, a timeline, and a Voyant page, to help add a visual aspect to my research. The timeline gives a sense of temporality to the monuments and attempts to humanize them a little more. The map is meant to be an interactive way of moving around the site. It includes the main motifs of the monuments, and the images and inscriptions of all of them. I used Voyant to highlight the use of language that revolves around the cemetery. I used the paper that I wrote on the cemetery to find the words that I used the most to describe it. I found when I was researching the cemetery, that I had to go to multiple sources to find all of the information that I needed. The goal of creating this website was to make more of that information available in one place.
