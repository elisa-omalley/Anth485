Victoria Past and Present

~ an augmented reality experience ~

Posted by Elisa O'Malley on December 4th, 2017

This project began from an idea that my classmates and I came up with for our "Bridging Victoria" exhibit at the Royal BC Museum. For our portion of the exhibit, we used the app Aurasma to make our poster come to life. I decided to try to do a similar project for my final Anth 485 portfolio project, the difference being the use of my blog as the platform to show ten images instead of three. I chose the images because they are places in Victoria I walk by almost every day. They are places I almsot take for granted without thinking about their history and who may have once resided there. Aurasma is an interesting plateform to use for comparison of images, and it adds a more engaging aspect to any research.

Step One: Download the Aurasma App from the App Store.

Step Two: Create a new account.

Step Three: Follow the user: "elisaomalley"

Step Four: Move your camera over the images below, and watch them come to life in the present.
